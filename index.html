Know Your Ingredients

Four forms, one source of flavour

Whole pod

Vanilla bean pods

The whole cured fruit of a climbing orchid, hand-pollinated and slow-cured for months — the purest, most flavourful form of vanilla.

Split, scrape, and infuse the pod in milk, cream, or syrup.

Pure seeds

Vanilla caviar

The tiny fragrant seeds scraped from the pod — maximum aroma and those signature black flecks, with none of the pod's woodiness.

Stir straight into custards, whipped cream, or glazes.

Spoonable

Vanilla bean paste with honey

A thick blend of vanilla extract and real scraped seeds — the flecks and depth of a whole pod, with the ease of a jar.

Measure by the spoon, straight into batters and frostings.

Stone-milled

Cocoa powder

Whole Madagascar cacao beans, roasted and stone-milled into a fine powder — pure, single-origin, and unsweetened.

Bloom in a little hot liquid to unlock its full aroma.
